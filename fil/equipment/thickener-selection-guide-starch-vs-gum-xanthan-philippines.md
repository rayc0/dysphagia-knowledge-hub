# Thickener Selection Guide for Philippine Patients — Starch vs Gum vs Xanthan

> **TL;DR:** Gum-based thickeners (xanthan, guar) are better for Philippine climate — they stay stable in heat/humidity and don't change taste. Starch thickeners are cheaper but break down easily. Available in PH: Nestlé ThickenUp (via Lazada/Digitindahan) and Unilab Smooth (local brand). Buy from hospital pharmacies for best quality and advice. Always check IDDSI level on the label.

## What Is a Food Thickener and Why Do Dysphagia Patients Need One?

A food thickener is a powder you mix into liquids (juice, broth, water, milk) or soft foods to make them thicker and slower to swallow. When someone has dysphagia — difficulty swallowing — their throat muscles may not work fast enough to stop thin liquids from going into the lungs instead of the stomach. A thicker liquid gives their muscles extra time to react and protect the airway.

The **International Dysphagia Diet Standardisation Initiative (IDDSI)** created a standard system with 8 thickness levels (0–7). Thickeners help patients move from thinner levels (like thin liquids) to a safer thickness level recommended by their doctor or speech-language pathologist (SLP).

## Main Types of Thickeners: Starch vs Gum

There are two main families of food thickeners used in the Philippines:

### Type 1: Starch-Based Thickeners (Modified Maize/Tapioca Starch)

**What they are:** Made from corn or tapioca starch that has been chemically modified to thicken liquids quickly without clumping.

**Pros:**
- **Cheapest option** — usually ₱300–500 per 100–200g container (retail)
- Easy to find in regular drugstores
- Work quickly at room temperature
- Familiar taste to Filipino families

**Cons:**
- **Taste changes** — can make juice taste starchy or grainy
- **Break down over time** — if left in a cup for >2–3 hours, they thin out again (especially in hot weather)
- **Temperature sensitive** — lose thickness when heated or chilled; can separate and become watery
- **Glycemic impact** — some contain added glucose, risky for diabetic patients
- **Don't work well in acidic drinks** — lemon juice, orange juice, vinegar-based broths can break down starch thickeners

**Best for:** Budget-conscious families, short-term use (single meals), room-temperature foods only.

---

### Type 2: Gum-Based Thickeners (Xanthan Gum, Guar Gum, Locust Bean Gum)

**What they are:** Natural or semi-synthetic gums extracted from seeds or bacteria. Most common in clinical settings: **xanthan gum** (most stable), **guar gum** (cheaper), **locust bean gum** (slower acting).

**Pros:**
- **Temperature stable** — work at hot or cold temperatures without thinning
- **Never break down** — stay thick for hours, even at room temperature in hot Philippine weather
- **Neutral taste** — no starchy flavor; juice tastes like juice
- **Work in acidic drinks** — safe for citrus, tomato, vinegar-based broths; won't separate
- **Diabetic-friendly** — no glucose impact
- **Humidity resistant** — don't clump or absorb moisture in tropical climate
- **Precise IDDSI levels** — easy to dial in exactly the thickness level the doctor ordered

**Cons:**
- **More expensive** — ₱600–1,500 per 125g (international brands); local brands may be cheaper
- Less familiar to families; may require SLP or nurse guidance first
- Take 1–2 minutes longer to thicken (need to stir well)
- Some brands have slight vanilla or neutral flavor added

**Best for:** Long-term care, tropical climate (Philippines heat/humidity), patients on multiple medications (temperature-sensitive), diabetics, quality of life (taste preservation), precise IDDSI compliance.

## Why Tropical Climate Matters for Thickener Choice in the Philippines

The Philippines has a **tropical climate with high humidity (70–90%) and temperatures 25–35°C year-round**. This creates two problems for thickeners:

### Problem 1: Heat & Humidity Breakdown

**Starch thickeners suffer in heat.** When a patient's meal sits at room temperature in the afternoon heat (or in a warm room without air conditioning), starch-based thickeners can:
- Lose thickness and separate back into thin liquid within 1–2 hours
- Become grainy or clumpy as moisture is absorbed
- Taste worse as temperature changes

**Gum-based thickeners (especially xanthan) stay stable.** Research shows xanthan gum maintains its thickness across temperatures (even 25–35°C) and doesn't absorb moisture from humid air. This is critical in rural areas or homes without refrigeration.

### Problem 2: Storage & Shelf Life

**Starch powders** in tropical climates:
- Can absorb moisture and clump in 3–6 months if not in a sealed container
- May develop mold if stored in humid bathrooms or kitchens
- Lose effectiveness faster than gum powders

**Gum powders** stay dry and effective longer because they don't absorb humidity the same way. Xanthan gum can remain stable for 2+ years if stored in an airtight container in a cool, dry place.

**Recommendation for Philippines:** If you live outside air-conditioned areas or in provinces with high humidity, choose gum-based thickeners even if they cost more — they'll last longer and work better.

## How to Choose the Right Thickener for Your Patient

### Ask These 5 Questions:

**1. What IDDSI level did the doctor order?**
- If Level 1–2 (slightly thick): Starch thickeners work fine
- If Level 3–4 (moderately to extremely thick): Gum works better and lasts longer
- If not sure, ask the SLP or doctor for a written prescription with the exact IDDSI level

**2. How long will the patient use a thickener?**
- Short-term (< 3 months, e.g., recovering from stroke): Starch is OK
- Long-term (> 6 months, e.g., Parkinson's, dementia): Gum is worth the cost

**3. Does the patient have diabetes?**
- YES → Use gum thickeners (xanthan, guar) — no glucose
- NO → Either type is fine

**4. Where will the patient eat meals?**
- Air-conditioned home or hospital: Either type
- Warm, humid home (no AC): Choose gum — stays stable in heat
- Outdoor or changing temperatures: Gum only

**5. Budget?**
- Very tight budget (< ₱500/month): Starch is your choice
- Moderate budget (₱500–1,000/month): Buy gum for long-term care, starch for emergency backup
- Can afford premium care: Xanthan gum blend (like ThickenUp Clear) for best taste + stability

## Mixing and Storage Tips for the Philippine Climate

### How to Mix Thickener Correctly

**1. Use clean, room-temperature liquid first**
- Pour ½ cup of juice, broth, or water into a cup
- Add ONE teaspoon of powder (check package for exact amount)
- Stir vigorously for 30–60 seconds until smooth — no lumps

**2. Let it sit for 1–2 minutes**
- Gum thickeners especially need time to activate
- Stir again before serving

**3. Add to the rest of the liquid**
- Once smooth, pour into the full cup or bowl
- Stir gently

**4. Serve immediately**
- Best thickness is achieved in first 10–30 minutes
- Starch thickeners will thin after 2–3 hours in warm weather
- Gum thickeners stay thick for 4+ hours

### Storage in Tropical Heat

**DO:**
- Store in **airtight containers** (not the original paper package)
- Keep in a **cool, dry place** (pantry, bedroom closet, NOT kitchen near stove)
- Use **desiccant packets** (silica gel) — ask hospital pharmacist for extra ones
- Label the container with: **thickener name + expiration date + purchase date**
- Keep away from sunlight (use opaque containers if possible)

**DON'T:**
- Leave open containers in the kitchen (humidity + heat = clumping)
- Store in the bathroom (moisture from showers)
- Refrigerate powder (temperature changes can cause condensation)
- Mix in bulk "to save time" — mixed thickener loses effectiveness over hours

## Common Mistakes Philippine Families Make

### Mistake 1: Using Regular Cooking Starch or Cornstarch

**What people do:** Buy food-grade cornstarch (Maizena) from the grocery, thinking it's the same as a dysphagia thickener.

**Why it's wrong:**
- Unmodified cornstarch requires cooking to activate — just adding to cold liquid won't work
- No IDDSI level guarantee — inconsistent thickness
- Higher glycemic impact
- Doesn't dissolve smoothly; stays grainy

**Right way:** Buy labeled dysphagia thickener products only. The word "modified starch" or "instant" on the package is key.

---

### Mistake 2: Mixing Thickener in Instant Noodle Broth or Very Hot Liquid

**What people do:** Pour powder directly into a bowl of 90°C hot lugaw or broth.

**Why it's wrong:**
- Starch clumps immediately in extreme heat; becomes lumpy, hard to swallow
- Some thickeners (especially gums) can scald if too hot
- Temperature too high to swallow safely immediately after mixing

**Right way:**
- Let the hot liquid cool to 40–50°C (comfortably warm, test with finger)
- Mix powder in a cool liquid first, then add to warm food
- OR buy thickeners designed for hot foods (some brands specify this)

---

### Mistake 3: Assuming All Thickeners Are the Same IDDSI Level

**What people do:** Buy any thickener, assume it makes Level 4, and give to patient without checking.

**Why it's wrong:**
- One teaspoon of Thickener A might make Level 3; one teaspoon of Thickener B might make Level 5
- Wrong thickness can cause aspiration or choking
- Different brands have different thickening power

**Right way:**
- The **label** should say the IDDSI level when mixed as directed (e.g., "Makes IDDSI Level 3 with 1 tsp per 250ml")
- If label doesn't say, ask the pharmacist or SLP
- Always follow the package instructions exactly

---

### Mistake 4: Not Stirring Long Enough

**What people do:** Add thickener, stir for 5–10 seconds, then serve.

**Why it's wrong:**
- Powder settles at the bottom; patient swallows thin liquid first, thick sludge last
- Inconsistent thickness means patient may aspirate

**Right way:**
- Stir for **at least 30–60 seconds** for gum thickeners
- Stir **another 10–15 seconds** after sitting for 1 minute
- Stir again before each sip or spoonful

---

### Mistake 5: Reheating Thickened Liquid

**What people do:** Mix thickener into broth, refrigerate, then reheat the next day.

**Why it's wrong:**
- Starch breaks down completely with reheating (becomes thin again)
- Gums can separate if reheated multiple times
- Consistency is unpredictable

**Right way:**
- Mix fresh thickener for each meal — don't store mixed thickener
- If reheating is necessary (e.g., lugaw cooled overnight):
  - Reheat the liquid WITHOUT thickener
  - Let it cool slightly
  - Mix thickener fresh before serving

## When to Ask Your SLP or Doctor

Call your **speech-language pathologist** or doctor if:

- Patient chokes or coughs during or after meals after starting thickener
- Thickener is making the patient refuse to eat (taste problem) — try a different brand
- You're unsure of the right IDDSI level
- Patient has new symptoms like constipation (starch can cause this)
- Thickener clumps or separates no matter how much you stir
- You're moving to a new house without air conditioning — ask about gum-based alternatives

**Find an SLP in your area:**
- **Philippine Association of Speech Pathologists (PASP):** pasp.org.ph — has member directory
- **Major hospitals:** All have speech therapy departments; ask for a referral
- **PhilHealth stroke units:** Most publicly funded hospitals have on-site SLPs
