# Oral Nutrition Supplements (ONS) Guide for Filipino Patients with Dysphagia

> **TL;DR:** Oral Nutrition Supplements (ONS) ay inumin na puno ng nutrients para sa mga pasyenteng may disfagia na mahirap kainin. Kailangan mong i-screen gamit ang MNA-SF, piliin ang IDDSI-compatible ONS, at bantayan ang kati-kati (dehydration) sa ating mainit na klima. PhilHealth ay limitado ang coverage, kaya importante ang local product sourcing at consultation sa iyong speech-language pathologist.

## Ano ang Oral Nutrition Supplements (ONS)?

Ang Oral Nutrition Supplements (ONS)—kilala rin bilang "nutritional drinks" o "supplement beverages"—ay specially formulated na inumin na puno ng calorie, protein, bitamina, at minerals. Ginawa para sa mga taong hindi kayang kumain ng solid food dahil sa disfagia, kalusugan na problemang, o malalaking kailangan ng nutrients.

Ang isang typical ONS ay naglalaman:
- **200–400 calories** per bottle (237 mL standard size)
- **7–15 grams ng protein** — pag-alagaan ng kalamay-kalamayan (muscles)
- **Complete vitamins at minerals** — Vitamins A, C, D, E, calcium, zinc
- **Micronutrients** — para sa immune system, bone health, wound healing
- **IDDSI-matched viscosity** — para safe kumain para sa dysphagia patients

Sa Pilipinas, ang mga kilalang ONS brands ay kasama ang Ensure, Sustagen, Pediasure, at local alternatives tulad ng Flexi, Boost, at Nutren.

## Bakit Kailangan ng ONS ang Mga Dysphagia Patients?

Ang mga taong may disfagia ay nahaharap sa triple threat:

1. **Kahirapan kumain** — Di kayang lumunok ng solid food, kaya mas kaunting dami ng pagkain
2. **Mababang nutritional intake** — Ang regular soft foods ay hindi sapat sa calorie at protein
3. **Malnutrition risk** — Sa 52% ng elderly with dysphagia sa nursing homes, malnutrition ay makikita (Pham et al., 2024)

Ang ONS ay tumutulong sa:
- **Plug nutritional gaps** — Direktang calories, protein, vitamins
- **Prevent muscle loss** — Specially important para sa sarcopenic dysphagia (weakness + swallowing difficulty)
- **Speed up recovery** — Especially matapos stroke o hospitalization
- **Maintain hydration** — ONS ay may fluid content, helpful sa mainit na klima ng Pilipinas
- **Support oral care at immune function** — Vitamins C, D, zinc ay tumutulong sa wound healing at infection prevention

## MNA-SF Screening: Kung Sino ang Kailangan ng ONS?

Hindi lahat ng dysphagia patients ay kailangan ng ONS. Kailangan muna ng **nutritional screening** gamit ang **Mini Nutritional Assessment Short-Form (MNA-SF)**.

### Ang MNA-SF ay 6 tanong lang:

1. **Food intake** — Bumababa ba ang dami ng iyong pagkain?
2. **Weight loss** — Bumaba ang iyong weight sa nakaraang 3 buwan?
3. **Mobility** — Kaya ka bang lumakad?
4. **Psychological stress / illness** — May health problem ka ba recently?
5. **Cognitive problems** — Sharp pa ba ang iyong isip?
6. **BMI or calf circumference** — Ang iyong katawan ay normal weight pa ba?

**Scoring:**
- **0–7 points** = Malnutrition (kailangan ng intervention, ONS)
- **8–13 points** = Malnutrition risk (kailangan ng monitoring + ONS)
- **14+ points** = Normal nutrition (ONS optional, focus on texture-modified soft foods)

Ang MNA-SF ay **89% sensitive** at **82% specific** para sa malnutrition risk (Rubenstein et al., 2001).

**Importante:** Kung ang iyong EAT-10 score ay ≥3 (dysphagia risk), dapat may ENT/speech-language pathologist (SLP) evaluation bago mag-start ng ONS. Maraming SLP sa Metro Manila (UP-PGH, UST-VRP, Makati Medical) pero kulang sa provinces.

## IDDSI-Compatible ONS: Alin ang Ligtas?

Hindi lahat ng ONS ay ligtas para sa lahat ng IDDSI levels. Kailangan mong i-match ang viscosity (kati-kati) ng ONS sa iyong assigned level.

### IDDSI Levels at ONS Compatibility:

| IDDSI Level | Viscosity Range | Safe ONS Choices |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 0 (Thin)** | <1 mL in syringe @ 10 sec | Pure water, unsweetened juice, Ensure Clear (if available), plain milk |
| **Level 1 (Slightly thick)** | 1–4 mL | Thin ONS products (Sustagen Junior, diluted Ensure 50:50) |
| **Level 2 (Mildly thick)** | 4–8 mL | Standard ONS (Ensure, Pediasure, Flexi, Sustagen)—no thickener added |
| **Level 3 (Moderately thick)** | 8+ mL | Standard ONS + optional mild thickener (cornstarch or modified food starch) |
| **Level 4 (Extremely thick)** | Sits as mound on fork | ONS + heavy thickener powder, OR specialty pureed ONS products (not common in PH) |

**Key point:** Most commercial ONS are naturally **Level 2–3** (mildly to moderately thick) dahil sa kanilang natural protein + carbohydrate content. Kung Level 0 o Level 1 ang kailangan mo, dapat:
- I-dilute ang ONS 1:1 or 1:2 with water
- Pumili ng "pre-thickened light" variants (kung available)
- Consult ang iyong SLP para sa safe thickening agent

**⚠️ WARNING:** Kung mag-add ng thickener powder sa ONS, check ang label. Ang *almagate, ibuprofen, at macrogol* ay incompatible sa xanthan gum-based thickeners (Freire et al., 2021). Mag-consult sa pharmacist kung may medication.

## Paano Gumagamit ng ONS: Practical Tips

### 1. **Timing at Frequency**
- **3 × daily** (ideal) = 1 bottle every meal, OR
- **2 × daily** (minimum) = 1 bottle every other meal, OR
- **As snack** = 1 bottle between meals para sa extra calories

Halimbawa:
```
Breakfast 7am:   Soft porridge + 1 bottle Ensure
Snack 10am:      (none)
Lunch 12nn:      Minced & moist meal (IDDSI Level 5) + 1 bottle Sustagen
Snack 3pm:       1 bottle Ensure or Boost
Dinner 6pm:      Soft meal (IDDSI Level 6) + (optional)
Bedtime 8pm:     Warm milk (plain or + powder thickener if needed)
```

### 2. **Temperature at Storage**
- **Room temperature** — drink as-is from bottle
- **Chilled** — refrigerate after opening; drink within 24 hours (tropical heat = bacteria growth faster)
- **Heated** — warm sa warm water bago mag-blend sa thickener (don't microwave sealed bottle—risk of explosion)
- **Opened bottle** — must finish within 24 hours kahit refrigerated; mainit + humid ang climate natin

### 3. **Blending with Thickener**
Kung kailangan mo ng thickener (IDDSI Level 3–4):

```
1. Pour 150 mL ng ONS sa cup
2. Add 1–2 tsp modified food starch (cornstarch brand)
3. Stir well para maiwasan ang lumps
4. Wait 2–3 minutes para ma-set ang consistency
5. Test sa spoon—dapat hindi mag-drip quickly
6. I-check vs IDDSI standard fork/spoon tilt test
```

**Tip:** Gumawa ng batch sa morning para sa buong araw; store sa refrigerator.

### 4. **Common Flavor + Sensory Issues**
Maraming patients ay "tired" ng lasa ng ONS after 2–3 weeks. Tips:

- **Rotate brands** — Ensure Monday–Wednesday, Sustagen Thursday–Friday, etc.
- **Mix with other foods** — blend ONS sa soft porridge, custard, pudding
- **Add vanilla or cocoa powder** — para sa variety (if allowed ng SLP)
- **Serve cold vs warm** — some patients prefer cold (refreshing), others prefer warm (feels like "real" food)
- **Smaller sips** — drink 50 mL every 30 min instead of 237 mL at once (easier on stomach)

## Dehydration Prevention: Why It Matters sa Pilipinas

Ang dysphagia + mainit na klima = **very high dehydration risk**. Bakit?

1. **Restricted fluid intake** — dysphagia patients ay takot uminom ng liquids dahil aspiration risk
2. **High sweating** — tropical heat natin ay nagpapataas ng fluid loss
3. **Thickened fluids** — mas hindi kain-kumain, so lower total fluid intake
4. **Reduced kidney function** — elderly patients ay may slower kidney function

### ONS as Hydration Tool:
- Ang 1 bottle ng ONS = ~237 mL fluid
- 3 bottles daily = ~700 mL fluid (+ 3 cups plain water/thickened water = 750 mL) = **total 1,450 mL/day**
- Target: minimum **1,500–2,000 mL/day** para sa healthy elderly; **2,000–2,500 mL/day** kung active

### Dehydration Signs (Bantayan!):
- **Dark urine** — dapat pale yellow
- **Dry mouth** — woke up with parched throat
- **Headache + dizziness** — especially morning
- **Constipation** — very common sa dysphagia + low fluid
- **Rapid heartbeat** — during normal activity
- **Confusion** — elderly may present confusion instead of thirst

**Prevention checklist:**
- ✅ 3 bottles ONS daily
- ✅ 3–4 cups thickened water
- ✅ Wet mouth after meals
- ✅ Daily urine color check
- ✅ Weigh daily — if lose >2 kg in 1 week = dehydration suspected

## Common Mistakes with ONS (Avoid These!)

| Mistake | Why Bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Only ONS, no real food** | Boring + expensive; patients stop drinking after 2 weeks | Combine soft foods + ONS (70% soft food, 30% ONS) |
| **No MNA-SF screening** | May magbigay ONS sa hindi kailangan; waste of money | Screen lahat bago mag-prescribe |
| **Wrong IDDSI level** | Aspiration risk if Level 4 patient drinking Level 0 ONS | Match viscosity sa assigned IDDSI level |
| **Don't check thickener compatibility** | Thickener + ONS may separate = poor consistency | Test sa spoon first before drinking |
| **Forget to refrigerate opened bottle** | Bacteria growth → food poisoning → vomiting, diarrhea | Finish opened ONS within 24 hours; refrigerate immediately |
| **No monitoring ng appetite** | Patient loses appetite sa regular food → malnourished anyway | If appetite ↓, ask: too much ONS? Bad taste? Need flavor change? |
| **Assume PhilHealth covers ONS** | Out-of-pocket cost = family stops buying after 1 month | Check PhilHealth eligibility first; budget accordingly |
| **Don't track dehydration** | Patient becomes dehydrated → weakness, UTI → hospitalization | Weigh daily; check urine color; log fluid intake |

## When to Ask Your SLP or Nutritionist

🚨 **Stop ONS and consult immediately if:**
- Nausea o vomiting pagkatapos uminom ng ONS
- Diarrhea or constipation lasting >3 days
- Aspiration signs: cough/wet voice after drinking, fever
- Severe bloating or gas
- Allergic reaction: rash, swelling, itching

✅ **Ask your SLP about:**
- Correct IDDSI level para sa inyo
- Best thickener brand sa Pilipinas
- Safe ONS options para sa inyong dysphagia type
- Rehabilitation exercises (Shaker, Mendelsohn) na pwedeng gawin habang nag-ONS
- When you can upgrade to softer textures (IDDSI progression)
