## Why we're publishing this for free

Most dysphagia information lives behind paywalls, in 200-page government PDFs, or in clinical jargon. Caregivers — the people who actually feed someone with dysphagia three times a day — rarely have time to wade through that.

We sell food. But before someone buys an IDDSI Level 4 puréed meal, they need to **know what IDDSI Level 4 means**, **why it matters**, and **what happens if you get it wrong**. The clearer that knowledge is, the safer everyone eats — and yes, the more likely they trust us when they need a supplier.

That's our bet: **be the most useful free resource on the internet, and the commercial relationship follows.**

## Editorial independence

This hub publishes information on standards (IDDSI, HK 護食標準, NHS, AHA, JSDR, etc.) that we did not create. Our editorial position is:

- Information is **descriptive** of what the standards say, not advocacy for our products
- We **cite primary sources** (the standards bodies themselves) so readers can verify
- Where standards conflict, we **show the conflict** rather than pick a winner
- Our products appear in **dedicated commercial sections only**, never inside educational content
- We accept **Pull Requests from anyone** — including competitors — if their corrections improve accuracy
